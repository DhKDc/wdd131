Rancagua, Chile

Rancagua, just a short trip south of Santiago, is a dynamic city and the capital of Chile's O'Higgins Region. It's a major hub for mining, thanks to the nearby El Teniente copper mine, and boasts a thriving agricultural sector, especially known for its vineyards. Modern landmarks like the Estadio El Teniente stadium draw crowds, while the city also offers attractions like the Plaza de Los Héroes and the Catedral de Rancagua. With its convenient location, blend of industry, agriculture, and urban amenities, Rancagua is a growing city in central Chile.
